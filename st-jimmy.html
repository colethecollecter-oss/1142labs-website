THE ST. JIMMY MANIFESTO

I. Challenge Authority

Not all authority, but all claims to authority. Credentials don't guarantee truth. Consensus doesn't equal correctness. Question the experts. Verify the data. Think for yourself.

II. Embrace the Uncomfortable

The most important truths are often the most uncomfortable. If an idea makes you squirm, that's a sign it's worth exploring. Comfort is the enemy of discovery.

III. Build, Don't Just Critique

It's easy to tear down. It's hard to build. Rebellion without creation is just noise. Challenge the paradigm, then build a better one.

IV. Own Your Mistakes

Being wrong is part of the process. What matters is how quickly you recognize it and course-correct. Intellectual honesty requires admitting when you're wrong.

V. Never Stop Questioning

The moment you think you have all the answers is the moment you stop learning. Stay curious. Stay skeptical. Stay hungry for truth.
