Major Depressive Disorder: Understanding the Darkness & Finding Light

What is Depression?

Major Depressive Disorder (MDD) is a serious mental health condition characterized by persistent feelings of sadness, hopelessness, and loss of interest in activities. It affects how you feel, think, and handle daily activities.

Depression is not simply "feeling sad" or "being lazy"—it's a complex neurobiological condition involving neurotransmitter imbalances, structural brain changes, and dysregulated stress response systems.

At 1142 Labs, we explore innovative approaches to depression treatment, including rapid-acting interventions and consciousness-based therapies.

Symptoms of Depression

Emotional Symptoms

  • ✦ Persistent sad, anxious, or "empty" mood
  • ✦ Feelings of hopelessness or pessimism
  • ✦ Feelings of guilt, worthlessness, or helplessness
  • ✦ Loss of interest or pleasure in activities
  • ✦ Irritability or restlessness

Physical Symptoms

  • ✦ Fatigue and decreased energy
  • ✦ Changes in appetite or weight
  • ✦ Sleep disturbances (insomnia or oversleeping)
  • ✦ Physical aches and pains
  • ✦ Slowed movements or speech

Cognitive Symptoms

  • ✦ Difficulty concentrating or making decisions
  • ✦ Memory problems
  • ✦ Negative thought patterns
  • ✦ Rumination and overthinking
  • ✦ Thoughts of death or suicide

Types of Depression

Major Depressive Disorder

Persistent symptoms lasting at least two weeks, significantly impacting daily functioning.

Persistent Depressive Disorder

Chronic depression lasting two years or more, with fluctuating severity.

Bipolar Depression

Depressive episodes alternating with manic or hypomanic episodes.

Seasonal Affective Disorder

Depression that occurs at specific times of year, typically winter months.

Postpartum Depression

Depression following childbirth, involving hormonal and psychological factors.
