WITHDRAWAL SUPPORT

Evidence-based protocols for safely tapering off methamphetamine, crack, heroin, and fentanyl. This is harm reduction, not judgment.

Why These Protocols Exist

Withdrawal is brutal. It's physical, psychological, and often isolating. These protocols are designed to provide a structured, evidence-based approach to tapering off substances while minimizing harm and maximizing the chance of success. They are not medical advice—they are tools for those who have no other options.

Methamphetamine

⚠️ HIGH RISK - Medical supervision recommended

Methamphetamine withdrawal involves severe dopamine depletion, intense cravings, depression, and fatigue. This protocol uses methylphenidate, cannabis, amphetamines (Vyvanse/Adderall), and LSD to stabilize neurotransmitter systems while gradually reducing meth use.

Fentanyl

⚠️ EXTREME RISK - Medical supervision REQUIRED

Fentanyl withdrawal is life-threatening and should NEVER be attempted alone. This protocol uses buprenorphine (Suboxone) as a safer opioid substitute, along with cannabis for symptom management. Seek medical help immediately.

⚠️ CRITICAL DISCLAIMER

These protocols are NOT medical advice. They are harm reduction tools based on research and personal experience. Withdrawal from any substance can be dangerous or fatal without proper medical supervision.

If you are considering withdrawal, please:

  • Consult a medical professional or addiction specialist
  • Consider inpatient detox or rehab programs
  • Have naloxone (Narcan) available for opioid withdrawal
  • Tell someone you trust about your plan
  • Call SAMHSA National Helpline: 1-800-662-4357 (24/7 free support)

You are not alone. Recovery is possible. Every life is worth saving.
