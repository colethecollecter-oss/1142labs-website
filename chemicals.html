COMPOUND PROFILES

Methylphenidate

Stimulant / NDRI
C₁₄H₁₉NO₂
Common Names: Ritalin, Concerta
Primary Action: Dopamine/Norepinephrine Reuptake Inhibition
Half-Life: 2-4 hours (IR), 3.5 hours (ER)
Mechanism of Action

Blocks dopamine and norepinephrine transporters (DAT/NET), increasing synaptic concentrations of these neurotransmitters in the prefrontal cortex and striatum. Enhances executive function and attention.

Subject of extensive 1142 research. Multiple papers explore therapeutic windows, withdrawal protocols, and cognitive enhancement applications.

Dopamine

Neurotransmitter / Catecholamine
C₈H₁₁NO₂
System: Mesolimbic, Mesocortical, Nigrostriatal
Primary Receptors: D1-D5
Functions: Motivation, Reward, Movement
Neurological Role

Central neurotransmitter governing motivation, reward processing, motor control, and executive function. Dysregulation implicated in ADHD, Parkinson's, and addiction.

Core focus of 1142 neuroscience research. Understanding dopamine dynamics is essential to cognitive enhancement and pharmacological interventions.

Psilocybin

Psychedelic / Serotonergic
C₁₂H₁₇N₂O₄P
Source: Psilocybe mushrooms
Active Metabolite: Psilocin (4-HO-DMT)
Primary Action: 5-HT2A Agonist
Mechanism of Action

Metabolized to psilocin, which acts as a serotonin 5-HT2A receptor agonist. Induces altered states of consciousness, neuroplasticity, and default mode network disruption.

Featured in 1142 consciousness studies. Research challenges conventional understanding of psychedelics and explores therapeutic applications.

Serotonin

Neurotransmitter / Monoamine
C₁₀H₁₂N₂O
Alternative Name: 5-Hydroxytryptamine (5-HT)
Primary Receptors: 5-HT1-7 families
Functions: Mood, Sleep, Appetite
Neurological Role

Regulates mood, emotional processing, sleep-wake cycles, and appetite. Implicated in depression, anxiety, and consciousness modulation.

Central to 1142 research on mood regulation and psychedelic mechanisms. Understanding serotonin systems is key to consciousness studies.

Norepinephrine

Neurotransmitter / Catecholamine
C₈H₁₁NO₃
Alternative Name: Noradrenaline
Primary Receptors: α1, α2, β1, β2, β3
Functions: Arousal, Attention, Stress Response
Neurological Role

Mediates arousal, alertness, and stress response. Critical for attention, working memory, and fight-or-flight activation.

Important component of stimulant research at 1142 Labs. Norepinephrine modulation affects cognitive performance and stress resilience.

Caffeine

Stimulant / Adenosine Antagonist
C₈H₁₀N₄O₂
Source: Coffee, Tea, Cacao
Primary Action: Adenosine Receptor Antagonist
Half-Life: 3-5 hours
Mechanism of Action

Blocks adenosine receptors (A1 and A2A), preventing adenosine-mediated drowsiness. Indirectly increases dopamine and norepinephrine activity.

Most widely used cognitive enhancer globally. 1142 research explores synergistic effects with other compounds and optimal dosing protocols.
