CHEMICAL DATABASE

11 substances studied. Molecular structures decoded. Pharmacological profiles mapped. This is the chemical foundation of 1142 research.

Studied Substances

Each substance in this database has been researched, analyzed, and documented through direct experience and scientific inquiry. These are not recreational drugs—they are tools for understanding consciousness, cognition, and the human mind.

LSD

C₂₀H₂₅N₃O

Psychedelic

Overview

Lysergic acid diethylamide (LSD) is a potent serotonergic psychedelic that profoundly alters perception, cognition, and consciousness. The cornerstone of 1142 research, particularly the 11:42 ritual.

Key Properties

Half-Life
3.6 hours
Duration
8-12 hours
Typical Dose
100-200 µg
ROA
Oral, Sublingual

Mechanism of Action

5-HT2A receptor agonist with additional activity at 5-HT1A, dopamine D1/D2 receptors. Reduces default mode network activity, enabling novel neural connections.

Research Applications

  • Memory archive access and trauma processing
  • Creative problem-solving and insight generation
  • Neuroplasticity enhancement
  • Consciousness exploration (11:42 ritual)

Methylphenidate

C₁₄H₁₉NO₂

Stimulant

Overview

Methylphenidate (Ritalin) is a dopamine-norepinephrine reuptake inhibitor used for cognitive enhancement, ADHD treatment, and neurodivergence support. Central to 1142's stimulant research.

Key Properties

Half-Life
3.5 hours
Duration
3-6 hours
Typical Dose
10-40 mg
ROA
Oral

Mechanism of Action

Blocks dopamine and norepinephrine reuptake, increasing synaptic concentrations. Enhances prefrontal cortex function, improving attention, executive function, and working memory.

Research Applications

  • Cognitive enhancement and focus optimization
  • ADHD and Down syndrome treatment
  • Synergy with psychedelics (Brainstorm Nights)
  • Weight management and appetite regulation

DMT

C₁₂H₁₆N₂

Psychedelic

Overview

N,N-Dimethyltryptamine (DMT) is a powerful, short-acting psychedelic known for producing intense visionary experiences and profound alterations in consciousness.

Key Properties

Half-Life
15 minutes
Duration
5-30 minutes
Typical Dose
30-50 mg
ROA
Vaporized, Smoked

Mechanism of Action

Potent 5-HT2A receptor agonist with rapid onset. Produces intense serotonergic cascade. Structurally similar to serotonin, enabling high receptor affinity.

Research Applications

  • Consciousness exploration and entity encounters
  • Rapid-onset altered states
  • Phenomenological research
  • Spiritual and transcendent experiences

Psilocybin

C₁₂H₁₇N₂O₄P

Psychedelic

Overview

Psilocybin is a naturally occurring psychedelic prodrug found in certain mushrooms. Metabolizes to psilocin, producing profound alterations in perception and cognition.

Key Properties

Half-Life
2.5 hours
Duration
4-6 hours
Typical Dose
15-30 mg
ROA
Oral

Mechanism of Action

Converted to psilocin in the body. Acts as 5-HT2A receptor agonist. Increases BDNF and promotes neuroplasticity. Reduces default mode network connectivity.

Research Applications

  • Depression and PTSD treatment
  • Neuroplasticity enhancement
  • End-of-life anxiety reduction
  • Addiction treatment

MDMA

C₁₁H₁₅NO₂

Empathogen

Overview

3,4-Methylenedioxymethamphetamine (MDMA) is an empathogenic substance that increases serotonin, dopamine, and norepinephrine release, producing feelings of emotional openness and connection.

Key Properties

Half-Life
8 hours
Duration
3-6 hours
Typical Dose
100-125 mg
ROA
Oral

Mechanism of Action

Releases serotonin, dopamine, and norepinephrine. Increases oxytocin levels. Reduces amygdala activity, decreasing fear response and enhancing emotional processing.

Research Applications

  • PTSD therapy (FDA-approved pathway)
  • Autism social anxiety treatment
  • Couples therapy and relationship healing
  • Trauma processing

THC (Cannabis)

C₂₁H₃₀O₂

Cannabinoid

Overview

Tetrahydrocannabinol (THC) is the primary psychoactive compound in cannabis. Produces mild euphoria, relaxation, altered perception, and appetite stimulation.

Key Properties

Half-Life
25 hours
Duration
2-4 hours
Typical Dose
5-20 mg
ROA
Smoked, Oral, Vaporized

Mechanism of Action

CB1 and CB2 cannabinoid receptor agonist. Modulates neurotransmitter release. Affects dopamine, GABA, and glutamate systems. Lipophilic, stored in fat tissue.

Research Applications

  • Pain management and inflammation
  • Appetite stimulation
  • Anxiety and stress reduction
  • Sleep aid

Ketamine

C₁₃H₁₆ClNO

Dissociative

Overview

Ketamine is a dissociative anesthetic and NMDA receptor antagonist with rapid-acting antidepressant properties. Produces dissociation, analgesia, and altered perception.

Key Properties

Half-Life
2.5 hours
Duration
1-2 hours
Typical Dose
50-100 mg
ROA
IV, IM, Nasal, Oral

Mechanism of Action

NMDA receptor antagonist. Increases glutamate release, activating AMPA receptors. Promotes BDNF release and synaptogenesis. Rapid antidepressant effects through mTOR pathway activation.

Research Applications

  • Treatment-resistant depression
  • Suicidal ideation reduction
  • Pain management
  • PTSD and trauma therapy

Methamphetamine

C₁₀H₁₅N

Stimulant

Overview

Methamphetamine is a potent central nervous system stimulant that increases dopamine and norepinephrine release. Highly effective for cognitive enhancement but carries significant addiction risk.

Key Properties

Half-Life
10 hours
Duration
8-12 hours
Typical Dose
5-20 mg
ROA
Oral, Smoked

Mechanism of Action

Releases dopamine and norepinephrine. Inhibits monoamine reuptake. Increases synaptic catecholamine concentrations. Potent euphorigenic and cognitive-enhancing effects.

Research Applications

  • Extreme cognitive enhancement (controversial)
  • ADHD treatment (Desoxyn)
  • Obesity treatment (historical)
  • Theoretical epigenetic research

Mescaline

C₁₁H₁₇NO₃

Psychedelic

Overview

Mescaline is a naturally occurring psychedelic alkaloid found in peyote and San Pedro cacti. Produces long-lasting visual and cognitive alterations with a gentle, introspective quality.

Key Properties

Half-Life
6 hours
Duration
10-12 hours
Typical Dose
200-400 mg
ROA
Oral

Mechanism of Action

5-HT2A and 5-HT2C receptor agonist. Phenethylamine structure (unlike tryptamine psychedelics). Produces visual enhancements, emotional introspection, and spiritual experiences.

Research Applications

  • Spiritual and ceremonial use
  • Introspective therapy
  • Addiction treatment
  • Consciousness exploration

Cocaine

C₁₇H₂₁NO₄

Stimulant

Overview

Cocaine is a tropane alkaloid that acts as a powerful stimulant and local anesthetic. Produces intense euphoria, increased energy, and heightened alertness.

Key Properties

Half-Life
1 hour
Duration
30-90 minutes
Typical Dose
20-50 mg
ROA
Nasal, Smoked

Mechanism of Action

Blocks dopamine, serotonin, and norepinephrine reuptake. Sodium channel blocker (local anesthetic). Rapid onset, short duration. High addiction potential due to intense reward activation.

Research Applications

  • Local anesthesia (medical use)
  • Addiction neuroscience research
  • Dopamine system studies
  • Reward pathway investigation

Fentanyl

C₂₂H₂₈N₂O

Opioid

Overview

Fentanyl is a synthetic opioid 50-100 times more potent than morphine. Used medically for severe pain but carries extreme overdose risk. Studied at 1142 in context of opioid crisis and loss.

Key Properties

Half-Life
3.7 hours
Duration
1-2 hours
Typical Dose
25-100 µg
ROA
IV, Transdermal, Nasal

Mechanism of Action

Potent µ-opioid receptor agonist. Rapid onset, high lipophilicity. Produces analgesia, euphoria, respiratory depression. Extremely narrow therapeutic window. High overdose fatality risk.

Research Context

  • Opioid crisis awareness
  • Harm reduction education
  • Memorial to Katrina (fentanyl overdose victim)
  • Addiction and loss research
