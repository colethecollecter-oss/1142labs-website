Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder: Healing from Trauma

What is PTSD?

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD) is a mental health condition triggered by experiencing or witnessing a terrifying event. It's characterized by intrusive memories, avoidance behaviors, negative changes in thinking and mood, and alterations in arousal and reactivity.

PTSD is not a sign of weakness—it's a natural response to overwhelming trauma. At 1142 Labs, we explore innovative approaches to trauma recovery, including the potential role of consciousness-altering substances in therapeutic contexts.

Core Symptom Clusters

Intrusive Memories

  • ✦ Recurring, unwanted memories
  • ✦ Flashbacks (reliving the trauma)
  • ✦ Nightmares about the event
  • ✦ Severe emotional distress
  • ✦ Physical reactions to reminders

Avoidance

  • ✦ Avoiding thoughts or feelings
  • ✦ Avoiding places or activities
  • ✦ Avoiding people or conversations
  • ✦ Emotional numbing
  • ✦ Loss of interest in activities

Negative Changes

  • ✦ Negative thoughts about self/world
  • ✦ Hopelessness about the future
  • ✦ Memory problems
  • ✦ Difficulty maintaining relationships
  • ✦ Feeling detached from others

Arousal & Reactivity

  • ✦ Being easily startled
  • ✦ Always on guard (hypervigilance)
  • ✦ Self-destructive behavior
  • ✦ Trouble sleeping
  • ✦ Difficulty concentrating

Treatment Approaches

Psychotherapy

Trauma-Focused CBT: Cognitive restructuring and exposure therapy

EMDR: Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing

Prolonged Exposure: Gradual confrontation of trauma memories

Pharmacological

SSRIs: First-line medication (sertraline, paroxetine)

Prazosin: For nightmares and sleep disturbances

Emerging: MDMA-assisted therapy in clinical trials

Complementary

Mindfulness: Present-moment awareness practices

Yoga & Movement: Somatic trauma release

Art/Music Therapy: Non-verbal processing

Experimental

Psychedelics: Psilocybin, LSD, DMT for consciousness work

Ketamine: Rapid-acting dissociative therapy

Neurofeedback: Brain training for regulation

The Path to Healing

Recovery from PTSD is not linear. It's a journey of integration, self-compassion, and gradual reclamation of safety and agency.

At 1142 Labs, we believe that trauma can be a catalyst for profound transformation. Through consciousness exploration, neuroplasticity, and evidence-based interventions, individuals can not only heal from trauma but emerge with greater resilience, wisdom, and depth.

You are not broken. You are healing.
